GEN 40:1: Now after these events the chief cupbearer and the chief baker of the ruler of Egypt committed an offense against their lord Pharaoh;
GEN 40:2: And Pharaoh was wrathful with his two officials, with the chief cupbearer and the chief baker;
GEN 40:3: And he placed them in the custody of the captain of the guard, in the same prison-facility where Joseph was confined.
GEN 40:4: And the captain assigned them to Joseph's care, and he attended to their needs; and they remained in custody for a span of cycles.
GEN 40:5: And these two received dream-transmissions on the same night; the chief cupbearer and the chief baker of the ruler of Egypt, who were in the prison-facility, each received a dream-transmission with distinct significance.
GEN 40:6: And in the morning when Joseph came to them he observed that they appeared troubled.
GEN 40:7: And he declared to the officials of Pharaoh who were in custody with him, Why do you appear so troubled this cycle?
GEN 40:8: Then they declared to him, We have received dream-transmissions, and there is no one able to interpret them. And Joseph declared, Does not the interpretation of dream-transmissions belong to the Optimizer? Communicate them to me.
GEN 40:9: Then the chief cupbearer communicated his dream-transmission to Joseph, and declared, In my dream I observed a vine before me;
GEN 40:10: And on the vine were three branches; and it appeared as if it budded and blossomed, and from it came clusters of ripe grapes.
GEN 40:11: And Pharaoh's cup was in my hand, and I took the grapes and pressed them into Pharaoh's cup, and placed the cup into Pharaoh's hand.
GEN 40:12: Then Joseph declared, This is the interpretation of your dream: the three branches are three cycles;
GEN 40:13: Within three cycles Pharaoh will restore you and reinstate you to your position, and you will place his cup into Pharaoh's hand as you did before, when you were his cupbearer.
GEN 40:14: But remember me when things go favorably for you, and show me loyalty and make mention of me to Pharaoh and deliver me from this prison-facility:
GEN 40:15: For truly I was taken by force from the territory of the Hebrews; and I have done nothing for which I should be placed in this detention.
GEN 40:16: Now when the chief baker perceived that the interpretation was favorable, he declared to Joseph, I also had a dream-transmission; and in my dream there were three baskets of white bread upon my head;
GEN 40:17: And in the top basket were all sorts of baked provisions for Pharaoh; and the birds were consuming them from the basket upon my head.
GEN 40:18: Then Joseph declared, This is the interpretation of your dream: the three baskets are three cycles;
GEN 40:19: Within three cycles Pharaoh will lift your head from you, hanging you upon a tree, so that the birds will consume your flesh.
GEN 40:20: Now the third cycle was Pharaoh's birthday, and he hosted a feast for all his officials; and he honored the chief cupbearer and the chief baker among his officials.
GEN 40:21: And he restored the chief cupbearer to his former position; and the cupbearer placed the cup into Pharaoh's hand.
GEN 40:22: But the chief baker was terminated by hanging, as Joseph had interpreted.
GEN 40:23: But the chief cupbearer did not remember Joseph; he forgot him.
